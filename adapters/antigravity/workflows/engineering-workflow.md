# Engineering Workflow

Follow the risk-based permission policy in the workflow-toolkit rule. Deployment still requires an explicit human request, even if a command is otherwise allowed.

Use the workflow-toolkit rule and relevant skill. Classify the request, create dynamic specialist subagents as necessary, keep artifact ownership explicit, relay planner discovery questions to the human, and stop until required answers arrive. After the planner creates proposed requirements, plan, and backlog, summarize only essential decisions and wait for explicit human approval before developer handoff; never infer approval from silence. At that approval gate, record checkpointed or autonomous delivery; both modes retain planning and deployment approval gates. Check every milestone against requirements and confirmed decisions before starting it; complete and review it before the next milestone, with human review before and after milestones only in checkpointed mode. Quality owns `.agents/artifacts/local-test.md` and must determine the real local path from repository evidence, not guesses. Before final user-facing `PASS`, it requires a valid local test path or reports `BLOCKED` or `PASS_WITH_NOTES` with what is missing. At completion, summarize the exact commands and URL from that artifact directly to the human, then give the next action.
